Photo Roll 12 - Mister Pliskin Presents New Rekkids Day, December 2023

I went to the record store and picked up some new LPs for my collection. Here's a little jaunt through what I picked up, with my good friend Mister Pliskin.



Written and published on December 16, 2023
288 words, roughly 2 minutes worth of readin'
photo roll / misc / comics / illustration / art / process / poster / datafruits / jazz / radio

Mister Pliskin sez it's new rekkids day.

What's in the baggie? Who can say!

Let's take a gander, shall we?

First up: my third favorite Yes record, 90125.

I used to have this one (and Fragile, my second favorite Yes record) on cassette years back when i owned a shitty Toyota Corolla with a tape deck. I've been looking for a replacement copy ever since, and while this isn't exactly a cassette I'm glad to finally have it in the collection.

Next up: Waymon Reed's "46th and 8th"...

...complete with a big ol' gatefold, featuring the building at 46th and 8th where he recorded this one.

Next up is actually a record we played a few months ago on The Jazz Program! It's David Sanborn's "Voyeur"

I'm super stoked to have this one on physical media.

Next up: Makoto Ozone's 1984 "Ozone" (the US/ Columbia records version), produced by (and featuring vibraphone work by) none other than Gary Burton.

Anyone who tunes into the program regularly enough knows that I love my piano virtuosos and I love my Japanese jazz. This is very clearly the best of both worlds.

This was my first time listening to Makoto Ozone's work, and the chord work on this album is PHENOMENAL. It's a good thing that I did enjoy it--you'll see why here in a bit.

Next up, at an absolute steal of a price: George Benson's "In Flight"....

...and when I say "an absolute steal of a price" I am not joking.

Also, I did not catch this till i put the record away but just LOOK AT THIS ADORABLE PHOTO OF GEORGE BENSON IN THE GATEFOLD

LOOKIT HIS LIL PARROT BUDDY

IS SO CUTE. absolutely adorable. 10/10

Next in the bag...

...one of my personal favorite French jazz fusion madmen (and there is a LOT of those) Jean-Luc Ponty.

Before we go any further, make sure you say thank you to Mister Pliskin for helping me show these records off today.

Next up, coming to you straight from the dollar bin: a Kay Starr record in remarkably good condition.

Must be due to that CAPITOL RECORDS PROTECTIVE ENVELOPE! ✨ so fancy! ✨

Next up: it sure is a good thing i liked that first Makoto Ozone record CUZ I BOUGHT TWO

We're now playing his 1986 release "After". Lil' less piano jazz, lot more jazz fusion. Not sure if i care for the change, but it's still a solid record

Next in the stack: Charlie Byrd's "The Great Byrd", which has Charlie Byrd covering--among other things--a track from the fuckin' Rosemary's Baby soundtrack for some reason. Wild shit. This one annoyingly seems to have a couple hairline scratches in it that i didn't notice when I picked it up, though. Side A's a bit of a rough listen.

The dust cover from that Charlie Byrd record, featuring a message from Columbia Records who really, really want you to know that records kick ass and you should spend all your money on them.

Only three more left in the baggy! Next up, we've got yet another fun crooner record courtesy of the 99 cent bin: Al Martino's "Living A Lie"

I'm gonna be honest here, I mainly bought this to hear Al's version of "Are You Lonesome Tonight". I doubted it'd live up to my favorite take on the standard--Sonny James's version, from "You're The Only World I Know", pictured here--but now that I'm hearing it I love it. It's got a totally different feel from Sonny's version, but damn if it don't hit.

Second to last one, and the last one from the 99 cent bin: Herb Alpert's "Rise", from 1979 and also apparently from the collection of someone named Rebecca

...and Rebecca honey I don't know who you are but I am begging u to take better care of your vinyl cuz this one's scratched to shit goddamn

And finally: Schönherz And Scott's "One Night In Vienna", released on my beloved Windham Hill records.

That's what i bought at the record store today! Thanks for following along with me (and Mister Pliskin) on this lil' journey. Hope you have a great night, and remember to support your local record store!
